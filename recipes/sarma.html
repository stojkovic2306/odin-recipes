Sarma

Go Home


Sarma is a popular dish of rolled leaves (usually pickled cabbage or vine leaves)
stuffed with a savory filling, typically minced meat (pork, beef, or lamb) mixed with rice, onions, and spices, then slow-cooked in a sauce.

Ingredients

  • Pickled Cabbage Wrapping
  • Ground Meant (Beef, Pork, Lamb)
  • Rice
  • Onions
  • Paprika
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Steps

  1. Prepare the Filling
    • Saut√© diced onions (and often garlic, bacon) in oil or lard until soft.
    • Add ground beef, pork, and/or ham, breaking it up as it cooks.
    • Stir in uncooked rice, seasonings (paprika, salt, pepper, Vegeta), and sometimes shredded carrots or potatoes.
    • Mix well and let cool slightly.
  2. Prepare the Cabbage Leaves
    • Use pickled cabbage leaves (from a jar) or steam fresh cabbage until pliable.
    • Carefully separate leaves and remove the tough central rib without tearing the leaf.
  3. Assemble the Rolls
    • Place a portion of the filling near the base of a leaf.
    • Fold the sides in, then roll it up tightly like a burrito from the base.
  4. Layer in the Pot
    • Line the bottom of a large pot with chopped sauerkraut, leftover cabbage ribs, or smoked meat.
    • Arrange the sarma rolls tightly in layers, seam-side down.
    • Add more smoked meat (like bacon) and bay leaves on top.
  5. Cook & Finish
    • Pour water or broth over the rolls to cover them.
    • Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 2-3 hours until rice is cooked and flavors meld.
    • Optional: Make a roux (flour, oil/lard, paprika) and stir into the cooking liquid for a richer sauce.
